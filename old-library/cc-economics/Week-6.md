## 16. Globalization and Trade and Poverty 10/10
1. What were some of the 8 Millennium Development Goals of the United Nations? ⁂
The goals included reducing child mortality, promoting gender equality, combating major diseases, achieve universal primary education, improve maternal health, combat HIV/AIDS, malaria, and other diseases, ensure environmental sustainability, develop a global partnership for development, and eradicate extreme poverty and hunger.
2. The **poverty line** is the minimum level of income deemed adequate in a particular country. Extreme poverty is a condition characterized by **severe deprivation of basic human needs**, including food, safe drinking water, sanitation facilities, health, shelter, education, and information. "Absolute poverty" is defined as living on less than **$1.25** a day. ⁂
3. What is "leapfrogging"? ⁂
Leapfrogging is the idea that countries can skip straight to more efficient and cost-effective technologies that weren't available in the past.
4. **Globalization** is the result of companies trying to outmanoeuvre their competitors. ⁂
5. Labour intensive products like shoes are often produced in countries with the lowest **wages** and the weakest **regulations**. ⁂
6. Who are the winners and losers of this process? ⁂
Winners: Corporations and their stockholders, consumers who get products at cheaper prices, and many workers in developing countries who often make above average wages for their region.
Losers: High wage workers in developed countries whose jobs moved overseas.
7. Opponents of globalization call outsourcing of jobs **"exploitation and oppression"**, a form of **economic colonialism** that puts profits before people. ⁂
8. What steps is the international community taking to protect workers? ⁂
Growing public awareness and pressure from the international community to take steps to protect workers. For example, the U.S. produces an annual publication called "The List of Goods Produced by Child Labour or Forced Labour".
9. Why did Muhammad Yunus win the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006? ⁂
He won for implementing microcredit - giving small loans, on average around $100, to low-income people in rural areas, mostly women, who used the money to start small businesses and raise their income.
10. Many of the people who have emerged from extreme poverty in the last 25 years have jobs, wages, and working conditions that would be **unthinkable** in the developed world. ⁂
## 17. Income and Wealth Inequality 8/8
1. How does wealth inequality differ from income inequality? ⁂
Wealth is accumulated assets minus liabilities - the value of stuff like savings, pensions, real estate, and stocks. Income is the new earnings that are constantly being added to that pile of wealth.
2. What is the "economic big bang"? ⁂
At first, countries' incomes were all bunched together, but with the Industrial Revolution the differences exploded. It pushed some countries forward onto the path to higher incomes while others stayed where they had been for millennia.
3. Most economists agree that **globalization** has helped the world's poorest people, but it's also helped the rich a lot more. ⁂
4. **Technology** has become a **complement** for **skilled workers** but a **replacement** for many **unskilled workers**. The end result is an ever widening gap between not just the poor and the rich, but also the poor and the working class. ⁂
5. What are some other reasons the income gap is widening? ⁂
The reduced influence of unions, tax policies that favour the wealthy, the fact that CEOs make salaries many times greater than their employees, and race and gender inequality can exacerbate income inequality.
6. What has been the time regarding wealth distribution in the United States between the poorest 20% and the richest 20% of Americans? ⁂
In 1970, the bottom 20% earned 4.1% of income and the top 20% earned 43.3%. By 2010, the poorest 20% made 3.3% of income and the richest 20% made over 50% of income.
7. Why do some economists today argue that the growing income inequality is harmful? ⁂
Countries with more inequality have more violence, drug abuse and incarcerations. Income inequality also dilutes political equality, since the rich have a disproportionate say in what policies move forward.
8. A **progressive tax** is a tax in which the **tax rate** increases as the taxable amount increases. ⁂
