## 3. Economic Systems and Macroeconomic 12/12
1. What are three things/questions each economic system social order has to figure out? ⁂
	Every economic system needs to determine what to produce, how to produce it, and who gets it.
2. The three major factors of production are **land**, **labor**, and capital. ⁂
3. What is a planned economy? ⁂
	A planned economy is one where the government controls the factors of production and helps decide what to produce, how to produce it, and who gets it through economic planning, usually in the form of some bureaucratic agency.
4. True Communism is a **classless society**, a social order where everyone owns the factors of production and output is distributed equally. ⁂
5. Socialism often has both **private** property and some **public** ownership and control of industry. ⁂
6. What is a free market/capitalist economy? ⁂
	A free market or capitalist economy is one where individuals own the factors of production and the government adopts a laissez faire or hands-off approach to production, commerce, and trade.
7. The invisible hand is the unintended **social benefits** resulting from **individuals and businesses seeking their own self-interest**. ⁂
8. What are some things that governments must do because free markets won't? ⁂
	Governments must maintain the rule of law through laws, police, contracts, and courts to keep everything orderly. They need to provide public goods and services like roads, bridges, education, and defense. They also sometimes need to step in when markets get things wrong by regulating pollution, worker conditions, and safety standards. 
9. Most modern economies are actually **mixed economies**, with both free markets and government intervention. ⁂
10. Summarize the circular flow model. ⁂
	The circular flow model shows how a modern economy works through the interactions between households, businesses, and government. Businesses sell goods and services to households in the product market, while households sell resources like labor to businesses in the resource market. Money flows in a circular pattern as households use money earned from selling resources to buy products, and businesses use money from selling products to pay for resources. The government also participates by buying products and resources, providing public goods and services, and collecting taxes from both households and businesses.
11. Why is the invisible hand of the free market seen as more adaptable to consumers than a government controlled command economy? ⁂
	The invisible hand allows businesses to quickly respond to changes in tastes and preferences because they have direct incentive through profit to produce what consumers want. Competitive markets create incentives for businesses to produce high-quality products efficiently, while government agencies would be slower to respond to consumer demands and less efficient without profit incentives.
12. If the free market is so advantageous compared to a command economy, why do we still need government intervention? ⁂
	Free markets don't account for externalities like pollution or worker safety, and consumers don't think about these factors when making purchasing decisions. Government intervention is needed to regulate production standards, ensure emissions and safety standards are met, protect workers, and address issues that the market mechanism alone cannot solve effectively.
