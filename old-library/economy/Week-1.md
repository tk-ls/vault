## Chapter 3
1. **Business loans are the best use of savings​ because they tend to expand production.**
2. **Loans made to businesses that do not succeed waste society’s store of savings​ and diminish capital​.**
3. **What is a consumption loan?** A consumption loan is one that **allows a person to consume today without having produced the wealth to pay for it**.
4. **What is an emergency loan?** An emergency loan is a loan taken out to **tide a person over during a temporary emergency**, but it still needs to be repaid through future production.
5. **If Baker and Charlie didn’t survive their sickness, the island would lose its supply of capital​ or its future production​.**
6. **What influences individuals and businesses to be more willing to apply for loans?** **Low interest rates** (easy credit).
7. **The imposition of a government layer in between savers and borrowers separates the cause and effect of lending, and leads to an over−allocation of credit​ or an increase in risk​.**
8. **Loans made to individuals or enterprises that do not succeed in creating a needed product​ or expanding production​ tend to weaken the overall economy by wasting the supply of savings.**
9. **Savings​ must be accumulated before it can be invested​ out.**
## Chapter 4
1. **Why didn't Able want to lend his net to the other two men on the island?** Able didn't want to lend his net because **he would not be able to catch as many fish as he could by using it himself** (it would lower his own productivity/consumption).
2. **What was the proposal Baker made to Able?** Baker proposed that Able **lend him the fish Able had saved** so Baker and Charlie could save time to build their own nets, and they would repay Able a greater number of fish later (with **interest**).
3. **Why was Able skeptical about this proposal?** Able was skeptical because **he worried that Baker and Charlie might not be able to pay him back** (risk of default).
4. **What did Charlie and Baker realize about this proposal?** They realized that **they would have to offer Able more fish back** than they borrowed (pay interest) to reward him for his risk and postponed consumption.
5. **What was Baker and Charlie’s new financial idea?** Their new financial idea was **borrowing with interest** (a loan).
6. **Describe 2 of the 4 options Able has when it comes to his surplus of fish.**
    - **Consume it.** (Eat the fish)
    - **Keep it/Hoard it.** (Store the fish for later consumption or emergencies)
    - **Lend it.** (Loan the fish for interest)
    - **Invest it.** (Use the fish to build a better tool)
7. **A desire​ for more is natural to all humans.**
8. **Able’s reward​ is his compensation​ for the risks he took.**
9. **Able’s ability to profit doesn’t hinder the progress​ of his peers.**
10. **Who takes the loss if Charlie and Baker aren’t able to make their nets work?** **Able** (the saver/lender) takes the loss of his capital, and Charlie and Baker take the loss of their time.
11. **Why is the charity option not as good for the economy as the loan?** Charity is not as good because it **does not require the borrowers to be productive** or to use the capital efficiently, and it **does not reward the saver** for his deferred consumption or risk.
12. **What is the bottom line?** The bottom line is that **wealth must first be created** (produced) before it can be consumed or transferred.
13. **The best thing about private capitalism is that it forces those who may only be motivated by greed​ to raise the well−being​ of others.**
14. **What is the “labour theory of value”?** The labor theory of value is the idea that **the value of a product is determined by the amount of labor used to produce it**. (The book argues this is false).
15. **What is the real reason the rich get rich?** The real reason the rich get rich is **through investment in productive enterprises that provide value to others**.
16. **When capitalism​ is allowed to flourish, productive capacity expands exponentially​.**
