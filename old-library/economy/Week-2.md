## Chapter 6
1. What problem did Max Goodbank solve and what was the revolutionary service?
    He solved the problem of storing and securing savings (fish). His revolutionary service was lending those savings (the fish) to borrowers.
2. Why would his profit potential be limited if all he did was charge a storage fee?
    The storage fee would have to be very high to cover his costs, and he would earn no profit from the productive use of the fish (saving).
3. What did Max do to increase his profit?
    He lent out a portion of the deposits (fish) to borrowers, earning interest on the loans.
4. Name 2 things Max needed to do in order to keep his loan business profitable.
    1. Charge an interest rate higher than what he paid depositors. 
    2. Ensure the borrowers paid back their loans.
5. What did Max offer to the most secure borrowers?
    He offered the lowest interest rates.
6. What did the loan rates determine?
    The rates determined which capital projects (nets, boats, etc.) would be undertaken, ensuring that only the most productive and profitable projects were financed.
7. Why did the interest rate system fluctuate? What influenced it? (one answer)
    It was influenced by the supply of real savings (fish) available for lending.
8. What would make the interest rates drop?
    A higher supply of savings (islanders choose to consume less and save more).
9. When did Goodbank have to be extra careful with loans?
    When the supply of savings was low, requiring him to charge higher rates and only lend to the safest projects.
10. What did Max do in order to compensate for the increased relative risk?
    He charged higher interest rates.    
11. **Higher** interest rates would discourage **consumption** and slow business growth.
12. What three things were involved in the cyclical interest rate mechanism?
    Saving, investment, and interest rates.
13. Deferring **current consumption** provided financing for capital projects that would increase future **productivity** and raise living standards.
14. Who was the choice when it came to bigger risks? Max Goodbank or Manny Fund
    Manny Fund (who often financed riskier, less established projects).
15. Who has set the base level interest rates for the past 100 years?
    The Federal Reserve (the Fed).
16. What are the two flaws of the system the Fed uses?
    Artificially low rates encourage borrowing and discourage savings.
17. When rates are too low relative to the supply of savings, what does this say to borrowers?
    It sends a false signal that there are ample savings available, leading them to undertake projects that are not economically sustainable (malinvestment).
