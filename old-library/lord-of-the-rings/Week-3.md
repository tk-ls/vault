## Short Answer Questions
1. Frodo putting on the Ring at Weathertop shows he's already losing control over its compulsive pull. Instead of protecting him, it exposed him to the Nazgûl and led to his stabbing, foreshadowing his ongoing struggle with its power. ==Frodo’s use of the ring reflects his temptation to escape danger but also his vulnerability to its influence, as putting it on ultimately exposes him to the Black Riders.==
2. Strider's knowledge of ancient lands, dangers, and hidden paths keeps the hobbits alive. His ranger experience allows him to navigate, find shelter, and anticipate threats that the hobbits could never have managed alone. ==Strider’s knowledge of Weathertop and other ancient landmarks allows him to choose paths that provide strategic advantages, such as better visibility, while avoiding known dangers.==
3. Glorfindel arrives from Rivendell, places the weakened Frodo on his horse Asfaloth, and ensures he reaches the Ford safely. His power also intimidates the Nazgûl, buying critical time. ==Glorfindel provides protection and speed with his horse, leading Frodo safely across the Ford while the Black Riders close in behind them.==
4. Elrond and Gandalf had prepared the river to rise against enemies. When the Riders crossed, a great flood swept them away, destroying their physical forms and ending the immediate threat. ==The river at the Ford rises up in a flood, empowered by Elven magic, sweeping away the Black Riders and saving Frodo from their attack.==
5. Those experiences were deeply traumatic and involved moments of weakness, particularly putting on the Ring. Reliving them meant confronting how close he came to becoming a wraith. ==Frodo hesitates because the memories of Weathertop and the Barrow-downs are traumatic, and he still feels the weight of the Ring’s influence on those events.==
6. Only Elrond's skill could remove the Morgul-knife fragment slowly moving toward Frodo's heart. Without him, Frodo would have become a wraith before the quest truly began. ==Elrond uses his deep knowledge of healing to save Frodo, showing his crucial role as a protector of Rivendell and the key figure in resisting the power of Sauron.==
7. Elrond's millennia of experience taught him the Ring cannot be used for good; it corrupts all who wield it. Hiding it is temporary; only destroying it in Mount Doom offers a permanent solution. ==Elrond knows that as long as the Ring exists, Sauron’s power will endure, and the only way to ensure Middle-earth’s safety is to destroy it completely.==
8. Frodo begins as a reluctant, comfort-loving hobbit guided by others. Volunteering at the Council, after all he has suffered, shows he has matured into someone who consciously accepts his burden out of quiet courage and duty. ==Frodo’s willingness to take the Ring shows his increasing sense of responsibility and bravery, as he accepts the burden despite his fear and doubts.==
