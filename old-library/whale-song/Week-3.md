1. **do you agree or disagree with the new plan to meet blue 55? do you think it will work? provide evidence.**
agreeing with the new plan is justified because iris shows determination and adaptability in the face of changing circumstances. the new plan to travel to lighthouse bay demonstrates resourcefulness and courage, and ultimately, it works—she succeeds in reaching blue 55, showing that persistence and flexibility often lead to success, even when the outcome is uncertain. the text says, “she wants to do everything in her power to let the whale know that he’s not alone... the plan is to take a train to cape Oliver and catch a shuttle to lighthouse bay”.
2. **significance of “the glacier screams, watching a piece of itself float farther and farther away from home.”**
this metaphor reflects iris’s feelings of separation and longing as she faces missing blue 55 and fears drifting away from her own purpose. like the glacier losing a piece, iris feels she may be losing a part of herself if she cannot connect with blue 55. it symbolizes the emotional distance she feels and her fear of being isolated or left behind.
3. **is iris determined? provide evidence.**
yes, iris is portrayed as deeply determined throughout chapters 33–48. she persists despite setbacks, such as blue 55 changing course and others doubting her plan. she creates her own speaker, devises new travel routes, and is willing to take risks. “she wants to do everything in her power to let the whale know that he’s not alone,” and she is actively involved in making things happen, not just hoping for results.
4. **journal entry from iris’s point of view (chapter 38)**
today, inspiration struck me when I saw the Bluetooth speaker. I realized I could use technology to help blue 55 actually hear my song in the water. I felt excited and nervous heading to the junkyard, hoping I could find the right pieces. as I put the speaker together, I felt more hopeful than ever that I could really reach blue 55. the possibility that I could make a difference and not be alone in the world, just like him, gave me new strength.
5. **how does Wendell’s e-mail about the giant planet relate to iris’s situation?**
Wendell’s email mentions a planet that went off its path and disappeared from the solar system, which resonates with iris’s feeling that her life, too, has been knocked onto a new course. she says, “I was on one path and something knocked me onto a new one,” highlighting the parallel between feeling lost and finding new directions and belonging.
6. **how is iris’s relationship with her grandpa brought to life?**
iris’s connection with her grandpa is made vivid through her reflections and her signing to Mr. Charles that “grandpa would want me to do for myself what I did for the whale.” her grandpa’s belief in her ability to figure things out and support others is a guiding force as she navigates her journey and choices, showing her personal growth rooted in his encouragement and wisdom.
7. **if whales could talk, what would this whale say to iris?**
blue 55 would express gratitude and relief: finally, someone listened. the text describes that when iris plays her song underwater, blue 55 hears “calls like his own... finally, someone was listening,” and he feels he didn’t need to be fixed. the whale would likely say thank you for seeing him as he is and trying to connect with him, ending his loneliness for a moment.
8. **analyse the mom’s reasoning for not wanting to send iris to Bridgewood. thoughts? provide evidence (chapter 46).**
iris’s mom is afraid that iris will be left out if she attends a deaf school, echoing her own childhood experience as a child of deaf parents who felt isolated. she says, “she’ll be left out if iris attends a deaf school, like she was as a child of two parents who were deaf.” eventually, grandma helps her see that iris needs to be happy, and iris reassures her mother that “she’ll never lose her.” this situation shows the generational fears around belonging and letting go.
9. **what does the ending quote, “a sound can move anything if it’s strong enough,” mean to you?**
the quote means that meaningful action, communication, or expression, even from someone who feels out of place or unheard, can create real change. just as iris’s song reaches blue 55 and bridges their loneliness, persistence and heartfelt efforts can break through barriers and bring transformation in the world and in relationships.
