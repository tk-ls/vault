## 1. why did Matthew react so badly when his dad redecorated his room?
in chapter 27, Matthew freaked out when his dad started painting his room because his dad almost painted over wallpaper lion, a piece of wallpaper that Matthew saw as a comforting companion. to Matthew, wallpaper lion was tied to his coping mechanisms for his OCD and anxiety. when his dad painted over it, Matthew felt like he was losing something important and safe, which made him panic and cry. ***Matthew reacts badly because his room is his safe space. When he sees all of his stuff moved around he is worried about the germs and worried about his things being touched and moved. Matthew is very upset that his 'friend' the lion is destroyed when cleaning off the wallpaper.***
## 2. why do you think Matthew is feeling so alone?
in chapter 29, Matthew tells his parents he doesn’t want to continue therapy, and his mum yells that he is “pulling this family apart.” even though he still has wallpaper lion’s eye in his pocket, he suddenly feels “very, very alone.” this shows he’s isolated both from his parents (who don’t understand him) and from the comfort he once got from wallpaper lion. his OCD is also making it harder for him to connect with others, so he feels cut off. ***Matthew is feeling so alone because he feels guilty for upsetting his parents. They tell him that he is tearing the family apart. He is also probably feeling alone because no one will believe what he suspects is happening with Teddy's disappearance.***

in chapter 33, when teddy mistakenly says “fishy” (pointing to Matthew) after being found, the police question Matthew. his parents step in and explain to the officers that teddy is only a toddler, with very limited vocabulary, so his words shouldn’t be taken literally. they defend Matthew and make sure he isn’t blamed without evidence. ***Matthew's parents support him by asking the detective if he has kids, and pointing out that toddlers don't really have a big vocabulary so they can't be expected to be able to answer questions honestly or accurately.***
