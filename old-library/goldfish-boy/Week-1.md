### How did the author make you feel panicked during the drowning scene? Use specific examples. Was it effective?
The author creates panic through several techniques:
- **Helplessness:** Matthew can see what's happening but is physically/mentally unable to act: "I could just run down, pull on my trainers, sprint over, and pull Teddy out... But I couldn't move."
- **Delayed adult response:** Mr. Charles is chatting with neighbours, oblivious to the emergency
- **Frantic pacing:** "THUMP, THUMP, THUMP!" - Matthew's desperate window banging
- **Time pressure:** The scene moves quickly from doll thrown in water to child falling in
- **Multiple perspectives:** Switching between Matthew's view, Casey's actions, and Mr. Charles's delayed realization
It's highly effective because it captures the reader's frustration alongside Matthew's - we want him to act but understand his paralysis, creating genuine tension.
***The author made me feel tension because you couldn't do anything but read as the boy was drowning. You really wanted Matthew to do something but he just banged on the window and shouted. The author made you feel anxious by using the words THUMP THUMP THUMP and hoping that someone would notice Matthew in his house. Matthew was also running around panicked which made you feel panicked too. It was effective because you felt helpless and that time was running out.***

### Inferencing: Why do you think Claudia calls Matthew the Goldfish Boy?
- He's constantly behind glass (his window) looking out, like a goldfish in a tank
- He's trapped in his "tank" (bedroom/house) and can only observe the outside world
- Goldfish are associated with being contained, watched, and separate from their environment
- The metaphor suggests he's on display for others to observe while being unable to truly participate in the world around him
- It emphasizes his isolation and the barrier between him and normal social interaction
***Claudia calls Matthew the goldfish boy and makes lip smacking movements like a fish would. Maybe she calls him the Goldfish Boy because fish are usually stuck inside of their bowls and can't go out and Matthew is always stuck in his house and doesn't leave. He just sits and watches everyone, just like a fish does.***
