## Make it satisfying: the cardinal rule of behaviour change
#### How did Luby enact change to a community aware of hygiene but not having a habit of being hygenic?
Dr. Stephen Luby studied communities in Karachi, Pakistan, where people knew that handwashing was important, but they didn't bother to do it. His team gave them Lux soap, which was a pleasant smelling, foamy soap that felt nice to use. Because the soap looked, smelled, and felt rewarding, handwashing became very satisfying for them. In the end, the habit of handwashing stuck, and rates of diseases went down. 
***The town understood that handwashing is important but people were not consistently doing it. He teamed up with a soap company that made good smelling soap. The townspeople began to enjoy handwashing and began to adopt the habit of practicing good hygiene. It was satisfying for them.***

## The cardinal rule of behaviour change
#### Summarise how humans deal with immediate rewards versus delayed rewards
Humans prefer immediate rewards rather than delayed rewards. This is because immediate rewards feel more satisfying because our brains evolved to value short-term survival. Delayed rewards are much harder to stick to because the payoff comes much later.
***Humans value the present more than the future so immediate rewards weigh heavier in decision making than long-term rewards.***
#### What are ways you can reinforce habits so that you'll want to repeat the behaviour?
You could attach an immediate reward to the habit, and use habit tracking to make progress visible and satisfying. You could also pair habits with something enjoyable, and celebrate small wins so the brain connects the habit to positive feelings.

## How to stick with good habits every day
#### Explain the "paper clip strategy" and the effect it has on making progress satisfying
One man had two jars, one with 120 paper clips and one empty. Each sales call, he moved one paper clip over to the other jar. Visually tracking progress gave a small, satisfying reward, and kept him motivated. 
***The paperclip strategy is a visual representation of the progress one is making. It is satisfying to see a jar fill up because it is a visual cue of the progress you are making to a habit.***

## How an accountability partner can change everything
#### What is a habit contract and how can it help create habits? What examples do the author give?
A habit contract is a written agreement stating your habits, goals, and consequences if you fail. This involves accountability partners who check on you, and makes bad habits unsatisfying. 
***Habit contracts are a way to begin putting your habit in action. Laws are a way that the government does it. Habit contracts can help speak a habit change into existence, as was the case with Bryan Hariss' fitness contract he had his trainer and wife sign. Once he reached those goals, he set higher standards to follow. Some people employ unsatisfying results if they do not follow through on their contracts, such as paying their trainer additional money if the goal is not met.

#### How does personality affect habits?
Your personality shapes which habits feel natural. Extroverts often enjoy social habits like team sports, while introverts might prefer solitary habits such as writing or reading. Aligning habits with your personality makes them easier to maintain.
***There are the big five for personality make up. Openness, conscientiousness, extroversion, agreeableness, and neuroticism. All of these can affect how one interacts with others, their goals, and themself. Choose habits and goals that suit you and work for you.***

## How to stay motivated in life and work
#### Describe comedian Steve Martin's habit that helped make him successful.
Steve Martin became successful by practicing every day for years, making small improvements to his act. His consistency and focus on gradual progress eventually led to mastery.
#### The goldilocks rule states that
We stay motivated when tasks are at the right difficulty—not too easy, not too hard. The “just right” level keeps us engaged and challenged.
#### How do variable rewards influence our motivation? What strategies do successful people employ? How can you use these for the habits that you are forming?
Variable rewards, like unexpected wins or feedback, keep motivation high because the brain enjoys unpredictability. Successful people use this by creating systems with small, frequent wins, such as progress tracking and setting milestones. You can apply this by building little rewards into your habits, like treating yourself after hitting mini-goals.

## The downside of creating good habits
#### What are the pros and cons of habits?
- **Pros**: save mental energy, create consistency, and build growth over time.
- **Cons**: can become boring, repetitive, and may cause you to stop improving once you plateau.
#### How to obtain mastery with habits?
Mastery comes from combining habits with deliberate practice. Instead of mindlessly repeating, you need to reflect, focus on weaknesses, and challenge yourself to go beyond your comfort zone. This balance keeps habits effective and prevents stagnation.
