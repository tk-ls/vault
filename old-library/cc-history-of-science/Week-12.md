## 34. Biomedicine
1. Biomedicine is **healthcare** based on a biological understanding of human bodies and **diseases**. ✓
2. The original wonder drug is a natural pain-relief compound of the **opiate** class that is still widely used today, **morphine**. ✓
3. What the life sciences had gained in **theories** and models, they still lacked in **applications**. ✓
4. A psychiatric discovery in 1933 was **insulin shock therapy** to treat **schizophrenia** and other mental disorders: patients were dosed with the hormone **insulin**, causing them to go into shock and then a coma. ✓
5. A **lobotomy** is surgically cutting connections in the brain's prefrontal cortex. ✓
6. The sulfonamides, or **sulfas**, were the first **antibiotics**. ✓
7. Penicillin treated sexually transmitted diseases, burns, heart infections, scarlet fever, pneumonia, and infections of the skin, mouth, and throat during World War II. ✓
8. Charles Drew realized that **blood plasma**, the clear part, without cells, lasts a lot longer than whole blood. He worked out how to **bank**, or store plasma for longer periods of time. ✓
9. German eugenics was the application of a distorted understanding of Darwinian evolution to human society, also called racial hygiene (Rassenhygiene). The Nazis believed that certain human groups were better than others, and that biology, not nurture, determined everything. ✓
10. During World War II, **Nazi** scientists such as Josef Mengele performed experiments on **humans** in **concentration camps**. ✓
11. Homeopathic remedies are based on the idea that a substance that causes disease in a healthy person can cure similar symptoms in a sick person. ✓
12. Doctors convened and created a new way of handling medical research with human subjects, the **Nuremberg Code** of medical ethics. In fact, a whole new branch of philosophy was born: **bioethics**. ✓
## 35. Genetics and the Modern Synthesis
1. The "modern synthesis" or "new-Darwinism" is a framework that uses Mendelian inheritance, Mendel's rules, to explain how Darwinian natural selection works in real time. It combines different ideas, from natural selection to statistics, accounting for change over time in species through quantitative analysis on different real-world populations. ✓
2. Thomas Hunt Morgan explored where genes are physically located on the **chromosomes** of fruit flies. The Fly Boys also created databases of different **alleles**, or versions of a gene. ✓
3. Theodosius Dobzhansky demonstrated that natural groups of flies have the same levels of genetic variation as mutants in labs, and that in the wild, variations are inherited pretty much as Darwin would have predicted. He joined the worlds of laboratory genetics and field naturalism. ✓
4. In his book, Dobzhansky defined **evolution** as the change in the **frequency** of an allele within a gene pool. ✓
5. Human "races" are not genetically defined, but **socially** constructed. ✓
6. Ronald A. Fisher showed **statistically** that what looks like continuous natural selection is actually the result of **combined changes** to many different genes. His work provided much of the foundation for **bio-statistics**, or how to apply statistics to biology. ✓
7. Population genetics is the study of how genes vary in populations, including models of how different alleles will change in a population over time. ✓
8. "Species" is defined as a group that can only **breed** with each other. ✓
9. "**Cline**" is the gradient of some trait within a population across a geographical range. ✓
10. Marxist principles state that there is only one, material world, and whoever controls the means of production, capital and technology, controls that world. ✓
11. In 1948, Trofim Lysenko talked Joseph Stalin into **banning** population genetics and other types of "bourgeois" biology entirely. ✓
## 36. The Computer and Turing
1. Historians often give credit for the first **mechanical computer** to the Artuqid-Turkmen engineer Al-Jazari, who died in CE 1206. ✓
2. In 1801, in the early days of the Industrial Revolution, French merchant Joseph Marie Jacquard incorporated the **punch card** into a textile loom to control patterns, arguably the first **industrial** use of computing. ✓
3. The basic idea today is that "computer" is a machine that can be **programmed** to perform **logical** tasks, like maths problems, **automatically**. ✓
4. Ada Lovelace wrote the first **algorithm** intended for processing using a computer, basically, the first **computer program**, in 1843. ✓
5. Starting in the late 1920s, influential American engineer Vannevar Bush created an **analog** computer called a **differential analyser**, which could solve **calculus** problems with up to 18 independent variables. ✓
6. A Turing machine is a thought experiment to figure out the limitations of mechanical computation. It can theoretically perform an algorithm, or programmed operation. It's a universal computer that could, given enough memory, accomplish any logical operation. ✓
7. The Germans used a device called an **Enigma machine** to create supposedly unbreakable ciphers, or ways of **encoding messages** so that only someone with the same cipher could read the message. ✓
8. The Turing Test is a game to test a computer's intelligence: a human asks questions to both a computer and another human, through a terminal, and tries to guess which is which from their responses. The appearance of intelligence is proof of it. ✓
9. The Church-Turing Hypothesis states that **computation** power is computation power. It doesn't matter if the power comes from electrical **circuitry** or a human brain, or how fast the individual parts of the machine are. ✓
10. Claude Shannon is most well known for publishing a series of papers after the war that founded **information theory**, which examines how information is encoded, stored, and communicated. ✓
11. Annie Easley developed computer code for **NASA** missions for decades. ✓
