1. What are the only two groups of animals that have perfected the art of biological sonar?
The two groups are bats and dolphins (toothed whales). Both have evolved similar sonar systems called echolocation to locate food and navigate their environments, despite living in vastly different habitats.
2. To date, what is the only sonar that the Navy has that can detect buried mines in harbours?
Dolphins are the Navy's only means of detecting mines buried in sediment. The Navy's Marine Mammal Program uses bottlenose dolphins with their biological sonar, which can detect mines that are tethered, resting on the seafloor, or fully buried in sediment.
3. Briefly explain how a dolphin produces its sonar?
Dolphins produce sonar clicks using their nasal-pharyngeal area. These sound waves travel through the melon, a fatty structure in the dolphin's forehead that acts as an acoustic lens to focus the waves into a beam. When echoes return, they are received through the dolphin's lower jaw and transmitted to the middle ear.
4. Explain how long-distance travel of spiders by 'ballooning' works?
Spiders climb to a high point, stand on tiptoe with their abdomen pointing upward, and release fine silk threads from their spinnerets. The silk catches the wind and, importantly, Earth's electric field provides lift. These electric fields can propel spiders and prevent the silk threads from tangling. Spiders can travel from a few meters to hundreds of kilometers, even crossing oceans.
5. What 2 chemical elements are found in earth's core and are responsible for turning the entire planet into a giant bar magnet?
The primary elements responsible for Earth's magnetic field are iron and nickel. These metals in Earth's liquid outer core create the magnetic field through their movement.
6. What is Zugunruhe?
Zugunruhe is a German term meaning "migratory restlessness." It refers to the restless behavior exhibited by migratory birds before and during their migration period, characterized by increased activity and orientation in the direction of their migratory route.
7. Where is Cluster N located on birds and what seems to be the main function?
Cluster N is a brain region located in the fore-brain of night-migratory songbirds, specifically in the visual Wulst. It processes light-dependent magnetic compass information received from the retina via the optic nerve and thalamus. This region is specialised for enhanced night-time vision and processes magnetic compass signals necessary for migratory orientation.
8. Which species of mosquito is responsible for spreading Zika, dengue and yellow fever?
Aedes aegypti, also known as the yellow fever mosquito or dengue mosquito, is the primary vector responsible for spreading Zika, dengue, and yellow fever viruses, as well as chikungunya.
9. Of the approximately 500 million neurons found in an octopus's central nervous system, how many are found in the arms?
Based on general knowledge about octopus neurobiology, approximately 350 million neurons are found in the arms, with about 150 million in the central brain. This means roughly two-thirds of an octopus's neurons are distributed throughout its eight arms, giving them significant autonomy.
10. The Scrub-Jay is responsible for spreading the seeds of which tree, which is the foundation of the ecosystem around them?
The Scrub-Jay (particularly the Western Scrub-Jay) is responsible for spreading oak tree seeds (acorns), which are foundational to many ecosystems. These birds cache acorns and often forget where they've buried them, leading to new oak tree growth.
