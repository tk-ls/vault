#immenseWorld
1. What is the meaning of the German term "Umwelt"?
The German term means "environment" or "surroundings".
2. According to Aristotle, how many senses are present in both
humans and other animals and which are these senses?
Five senses: sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch.
3. What is "Occam's Razor" principle?
A problem-solving principle that suggests the simplest
explanation is usually the best one.
4. How do tree-hoppers communicate?
They communicate through vibrational signals that travel along
the stems and leaves of plants.
5. What is the odour of isoamyl acetate?
Sweet and fruity, like a banana or pear.
6. What does leopard urine smell like?
It is widely described as smelling like buttered popcorn.
7. According to the author, what are the most ancient and universal
sources of sensory information?
Definitely chemicals.
8. What are pheromones?
A chemical substance produced and released into the
environment by an animal, affecting the behaviour or physiology
of others of its species.
9. What is Z-7-dodecen-l-yl acetate?
A chemical compound that acts as a sex pheromone in both
insects and mammals to attract males.
10. What is the role of the vomeronasal organ, also known as
Jacobson's organ, found in snakes?
It allows snakes to smell the air by collecting airborne chemicals
with their forked tongue.11. What are the 5 basic qualities/tastes in humans?
They are salt, sweet, bitter sour, and savoury.
11. According to Elizabeth Jakob, what type of spiders are the only
spiders that will turn and look at you routinely?
Jumping spiders.
12. What are ommatidia?
Light-gathering units inside compound eyes.
13. According to scientist Tim Caro, why are zebras striped?
To ward off bloodsucking flies.
14. What are the two major groups of animal photoreceptors?
Ciliary and rhabdomeric receptors.
15. Explain why human tetrachromats are usually women and not
men?
Because the genes for the long and medium opsins both sit on
the X chromosome.
16. How many minutes can the naked mole-rats survive without
oxygen?
Up to 18 minutes.
17. What is the abundantly clear evolutionary benefit of nociception
in animals?
It is an alarm system that allows animals to detect things that
might harm or kill them.
18. According to Ann Carr and Vincent Salgado's findings, from what
distance can ticks detect body heat?
Up to 13 feet away.
19. What are vibrissae more commonly known as, and what is the
origin of their name?
The term is more commonly known as whiskers, and comes from
the Latin word vibrare, which means to vibrate, because the hairs
transmit sensory information through vibrations that stimulate
nerves at their base.
20. What is a "hydrodynamic wake"?
It is a trail of swirling water that continues to whirl long after theanimal has passed.
21. What are "slit sensilla"?
Vibration detecting organs common to all arachnids.
22. Where are the ears of crickets and katydids located?
On their knees.
