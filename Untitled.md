**Part C: Summary**
Motivation is the need or desire to do something, and psychologists explain it through four main theories. The instinct theory suggests behaviors are innate, while drive-reduction theory says we act to satisfy physiological needs and maintain homeostasis. The optimal arousal theory proposes that we seek a balance between boredom and stress. Finally, Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs shows that we prioritize basic survival needs before higher ones like belonging, esteem, and self-actualization.

**Part C: Summary**
Developmental psychology studies how we change physically, cognitively, socially, and emotionally throughout our lives. Jean Piaget proposed that children develop through four cognitive stages, from the sensorimotor stage in infancy to the formal operational stage in adolescence. He introduced the concepts of schemas, assimilation, and accommodation to explain how we process new experiences. Lev Vygotsky added that social interaction and parental guidance also play a key role in early cognitive development.

The Harlow monkey experiment revealed that contact and touch, not just food, are vital to healthy attachment and development.
