“Me, We.”
Two words, four letters, one legendary boxer. In 1975, when Muhammad Ali spoke these words at Harvard, he wasn’t just giving a rhyme; he was summarising the entire human experience. For my speech, I want to talk about how the shortest poem in history carries the world’s biggest message.
Throwback to 1975, when Muhammad Ali was standing before a crowd of Harvard students. After a long, inspirational speech, someone shouted from the back: ‘Give us a poem!’ Ali didn’t hesitate. He didn’t reach for a book. He simply looked out at the sea of faces, and said: “Me, We.” In that split second, Ali bridged the gap between the individual and the community. 
We often think of the words “Me” and “We” as opposites. “Me” is about MY goals, MY aura, and MY life. “We” is about sacrifice, community, and others. But Muhammad Ali, a man known for his massive ‘Me’, showed us they are actually two sides of the same coin. His poem is a masterclass in how one person’s greatness only matters when it’s shared with the world.
