### 1. Problems They Helped Solve
The primary problem was **Information Chaos**.
- **The Mess:** In the mid-90s, the internet was a disorganized mess of pages.
- **The Failure of Others:** Early search engines (like AltaVista or Yahoo) ranked pages by how many times a keyword appeared. If a page said "pizza" 500 times, it was #1, even if the content was garbage. This was called "keyword stuffing."
### 2. Other Ideas to Solve the Problem
Before Larry’s **PageRank** algorithm, people tried these methods:
- **Human Directories:** Yahoo’s original idea was to have actual humans manually categorize every website into a "phonebook." This couldn't scale as the web grew by millions of pages.
- **Meta-Tags:** Engines relied on what website owners _claimed_ their site was about. This failed because people lied to get more traffic.
### 3. Early Version & Appearance
- **The Name:** It started as **Backrub** in 1996.
- **The Look:** It was incredibly sparse. While other sites (like MSN) were cluttered with ads, news, and weather, Google was just a logo and a search box.
- **The Hardware:** As mentioned, the first server was housed in a **custom case made of Lego bricks**.
- **The URL:** It didn't even have its own domain at first; it lived at `google.stanford.edu`.

### 4. Why Their Business Was Successful

- **Superior Tech:** PageRank was objectively better. It treated a link from one site to another as a "vote" of confidence.
    
- **The Business Model (AdWords):** They didn't just sell banner ads; they sold ads that were **relevant** to what you were searching for. This made ads helpful rather than annoying.
    
- **Scalability:** They focused on building a massive network of cheap computers working together, rather than one giant expensive supercomputer.
    

### 5. How They Made the World a Better Place

- **Democratization of Knowledge:** Before Google, finding niche information required a library and hours of research. Page made the world's information accessible to anyone with a flip phone or a computer.
    
- **Moonshots:** Through Alphabet, he funded research into **clean energy**, **self-driving cars** (to reduce traffic deaths), and **biotech** (to cure diseases).
