## 29. OCD & Anxiety Disorders
1. **Anxiety Disorders** are characterized not only by distressing, persistent anxiety but also often by the **dysfunctional behaviors that reduce that anxiety**.
2. **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)** is characterized by unwanted repetitive thoughts (which become **obsessions**) and are sometimes accompanied by actions (which become **compulsions**).
3. Being neat, orderly, and fastidious / washing hands compulsively / checking the stove constantly / counting steps.
4. **Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)** is defined as **continually tense and apprehensive**, experiencing unfocused, negative and out-of-control feelings.
5. **6 months**
6. **Panic Disorder** is defined by repeated suffering of **panic attacks**: sudden episodes of **intense dread** or sudden fear that come without warning.
7. Chest pains, racing heartbeat, difficulty breathing, feeling like you're going crazy or dying.
8. **Phobias** are persistent, **irrational fears** of specific objects, activities, or situations.
9. Every time you avoid or escape a feared situation, you ease your anxiety temporarily, which reinforces the phobic behavior through negative reinforcement, making it stronger.
## 30. Depressive and Bipolar Disorders
1. **Mania** is a mood disorder marked by a **period of intense, restless, but often optimistic hyperactivity with a skewed estimation of self and abilities** state.
2. Moods are **emotional states** that are even more subjective and harder to define than emotions themselves. The most important distinction between emotion and mood is that moods are **long-term emotional states** rather than discreet, fleeting feelings.
3. **Mood Disorders** are characterized by **emotional extremes** and challenges in regulating mood (tend to be longer-term disturbances).
4. At least five symptoms for more than two weeks: depressed mood, significant weight/appetite changes, too much or too little sleep, decreased interest in activities, feeling worthless/fatigued/lethargic, difficulty concentrating or making decisions, recurrent thoughts of death or suicide.
5. **Manic Depression** is the outdated term for **bipolar disorder**, which is a mood disorder in which a person alternates between the hopelessness and lethargy of **depression** and the overexcited state of **mania**.
6. The cause of mood disorders is often a combination of **biological**, **genetic**, **psychological**, and **environmental** factors.
7. Women may seek treatment more often than men; depression in men may manifest as anger and aggression rather than sadness, leading to underdiagnosis.
8. Norepinephrine is severely lacking in depressed brains but elevated during manic episodes; low serotonin levels correlate with depressive states. Most antidepressants work by raising serotonin or norepinephrine levels.
9. The social-**cognitive perspective** examines how our **thinking and behavior** and **learned helplessness/self-blame** influence depression.
10. Depressed minds view bad events through a negative internal lens, engaging in negative thinking, learned helplessness, self-blame, and over-thinking, creating a self-fulfilling cycle of negativity.
## 31. Trauma and Addiction
1. WWI combat (J.R.R. Tolkien), car accidents, natural disasters, violent crimes, September 11.
2. As nightmares, flashbacks, avoidance, fear, guilt, anxiety, rage, insomnia, and interference with daily functioning.
3. **Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)** was once known as **"shell shock"** and is defined as a psychological disorder generated by either **witnessing** or **experiencing a traumatic event**.
4. (1) Re-living the event through intrusive memories/nightmares/flashbacks; (2) avoidance of associated situations; (3) excessive physiological arousal (anxiety, irritability, sleep/concentration problems); (4) pervasive negative changes in emotions and beliefs (guilt, fear, shame, loss of enjoyment).
5. **Genetic predispositions** making some people more vulnerable than others to PTSD.
6. Both involve the limbic system flooding the body with stress hormones like cortisol, and the amygdala and hippocampus being involved in fight-or-flight reactions.
7. Treatment and social support help some sufferers achieve **post-traumatic growth**, which are the **positive psychological changes** resulting from the struggle with challenging circumstances and life crises.
8. **Addiction** or **dependence** is/are defined as compulsive, excessive, and difficult-to-control substance use or other, initially pleasurable behavior that begins to **interfere with ordinary** life, work, health, or relationships.
9. Over-consuming drugs or alcohol, compulsive gambling, eating, shopping, exercising, or having sex.
10. **Physical dependence** is a physiological need for a drug that reveals itself through withdrawal symptoms if use stops. **Psychological dependence** is the need to use a drug or complete an activity to relieve negative emotions.
11. The **dual diagnosis model** treats both the addiction and the underlying psychological disorder (e.g. PTSD) at the same time, rather than treating one before the other.
