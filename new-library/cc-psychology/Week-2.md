## 5. Sensation and Perception
1. Sensation is the _bottom-up process_ by which our senses, like vision, hearing and smell, _receive and relay outside stimuli_. ==The bottom-up process by which our senses, like vision, hearing, and smell, receive and relay outside information==
2. Perception is the _top-down_ way our brains organise and interpret that information and _put it into context_. ==The top-down way our brains organise and interpret that information and put it into context==
3. The Absolute Threshold of sensation is the _minimum stimulation_ needed to register a particular stimulus 50% of the time. ==Minimum stimulation==
4. Signal Detection Theory is a model for predicting how and when a person will detect weak stimuli, partly based on context. What example does Hank Green give to demonstrate this concept? Exhausted new parents might hear their baby's tiniest whimper but not even register the bellow of a passing train. ==Parents may notice their baby’s tiny whimper but not notice the passing of a train==

According to Weber's Law, we perceive differences based on percentage change, not absolute amount of change. ==The difference between being able to notice two things is based on a % difference rather than a absolute difference==

9. How does the Trichromatic Colour Vision Theory differ from the Opponent-Process Colour Vision Theory? The Young-Helmholtz Trichromatic Theory suggests that the retina has three specific colour receptor cones that register red, green, and blue, and their combined power allows the eye to register any colour. The Opponent-Process Theory suggests that we see colour through processes that work against each other. ==For trichromatic, colour cones of red, green, and blue activate to see all the different shades of colour; in the opponent-process theory, receptor cells are stimulated by certain colours and inhibited by others.==
10. What example does Hank Green give to demonstrate feature detectors and parallel processing? He uses the example of seeing a clown: "That clown is frowning and running at me with a tiny cream pie." The brain has specialised feature detectors that respond to specific features like shapes, angles, and movements. Through parallel processing, the brain simultaneously works on making sense of form, depth, motion, and colour. ==Your brain detects the features of a clown chasing you and does so all at the same time==
