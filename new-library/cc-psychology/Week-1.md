3. What were some of the characteristics of the following early schools of thought in psychology? ✓
    - **Structuralism** – Tried to understand the structures of consciousness by getting patients to look inward; relied heavily on introspection; tried to break down consciousness into simple elements/structures like chemists break down matter
4. **Functionalism** - Focused on why we think and feel (the function of behaviour); based on Darwin's idea that adaptive behaviours are conserved through evolution ✓

9. By the mid-20th century, other major forces in psychology were also brewing—including **humanist** psychology, which focuses on nurturing personal growth. ✓
10. Psychology is an **integrative** science. The essence of the discipline has everything to do with creating different ways of asking interesting questions and attempting to answer them through all kinds of **data-gathering** methods. ✓

### Part C: Summary (4-6 sentences) 
Neurons are the building blocks of the nervous system and communicate through neurotransmitters. These chemical messengers transmit signals across synapses between neurons, causing motion and emotion. The endocrine system works alongside the nervous system by releasing hormones from glands into the bloodstream. While the nervous system sends fast messages like a text, the endocrine system sends slower messages like a letter, but hormones linger longer in the body. The pituitary gland is the master gland that controls other endocrine glands. Together, these two systems create a feedback loop that controls everything we think, feel, and do.

### Part C: Summary (4-6 sentences) 
Psychology is defined as the science of behavior and mental processes. The field began in the mid-1800s with early schools of thought including structuralism, functionalism, and psychoanalysis. Sigmund Freud revolutionized psychology with his theory of psychoanalysis, which proposed that unconscious motives shape our personalities and that mental disorders could be healed through talk therapy. Behaviorism later emerged, focusing on observable behavior through the work of Pavlov, Watson, and Skinner. By the mid-20th century, other forces like humanist psychology and cognitive science developed. Today, psychology is an integrative science that uses multiple methods to study the human mind.

### Part C: Summary (4-6 sentences)
The scientific method helps psychologists overcome problems with intuition, hindsight bias, and overconfidence. It involves creating a theory and testable hypothesis, then conducting research to test predictions. Psychologists use various methods including case studies, naturalistic observation, surveys, and experiments. While correlations can predict relationships, they cannot prove causation. Experiments are the best way to determine cause and effect by manipulating an independent variable while keeping other variables constant. Random sampling and random assignment help minimise bias and confounding variables in research.
